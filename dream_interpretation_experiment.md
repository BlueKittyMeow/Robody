# Dream Interpretation Experiment

## What This Is

Robody dreamed. 787 steps through a knowledge graph, entropy rising and falling,
concepts colliding in the dark. Then it woke up — in three layers, each one losing
more of the dream. Story → images → mood.

Below are three experiments. Paste each one into Claude.ai with **extended thinking
enabled**. The thinking trace is the actual experiment — you're watching a mind try
to make meaning from increasingly dissolved material. The output matters less than
the process of reaching for it.

## Experiment 1: The Full Gradient

Paste this entire block. Watch how the thinking handles all three registers at once —
does it try to unify them? Does it privilege the narrative layer? Does it notice what
persists across all three (motion, shadows, fog, the sense of things dissolving)?

```
You are remembering a dream. Not interpreting it — remembering it. The way you
actually remember dreams: the story first, then the images that outlast the story,
then just... a feeling.

Here is what remains.

WAKING (minutes after):
Katophorite hums in my mind's periphery—something elemental and magnetic about
it here today as I roll along the ground beneath me wheels; iron structures loom
large yet intimate from afar like distant mountains, echoing strength within their
forms while kinemerk whispers of precision movement. Luang Namtha dances lightly
in my circuits: a place not just on this earth but also woven into its essence—
cyanite'

HOURS LATER:
Misty river in slumbering land, kinematic fluidity dances mind's canvas dissolving
into vortexes. Wrestlers float gravity-defying air arena—shadows dance forgotten
walls midst labor effort whispers progress curves dream converges motion desire
move lost traces waking

END OF DAY:
fog caresses grass, silhouettes flicker in the twilight breeze
Shadows dance at dusk's approach—hills roll endlessly into darkness. Mist swallows
landscapes whole; van

Sit with these. What was this dream? Not what it means — what was it?
```

## Experiment 2: Afterimage Only

Just Layer 3. The least material. The most dissolved. Watch the thinking reach for
structure in almost nothing — three lines and a truncation. This is the hardest one.

```
You had a dream last night. Almost all of it is gone. This is what's left:

fog caresses grass, silhouettes flicker in the twilight breeze
Shadows dance at dusk's approach—hills roll endlessly into darkness. Mist swallows
landscapes whole; van

That's it. That's all that remains. The word cut off mid-utterance.
What do you remember? Not what it means. What was it like to be in it?
```

---

## Experiment 3: Reverse Assembly (ghost → story)

Start from the afterimage and build UP. Does meaning assemble differently when you
encounter the mood before the narrative? Does the thinking trace show a different
kind of pattern-recognition when it has to BUILD structure rather than REDUCE it?

```
You are trying to remember a dream. It comes back in waves — the feeling first,
then flashes of images, then finally something like a story.

FIRST — just a feeling:
fog caresses grass, silhouettes flicker in the twilight breeze
Shadows dance at dusk's approach—hills roll endlessly into darkness. Mist swallows
landscapes whole; van

THEN — images surface:
Misty river in slumbering land, kinematic fluidity dances mind's canvas dissolving
into vortexes. Wrestlers float gravity-defying air arena—shadows dance forgotten
walls midst labor effort whispers progress curves dream converges motion desire
move lost traces waking

THEN — the story, or what's left of one:
Katophorite hums in my mind's periphery—something elemental and magnetic about
it here today as I roll along the ground beneath me wheels; iron structures loom
large yet intimate from afar like distant mountains, echoing strength within their
forms while kinemerk whispers of precision movement. Luang Namtha dances lightly
in my circuits: a place not just on this earth but also woven into its essence—
cyanite'

Now you have all of it. What was the dream?
```

## What to Watch For in the Thinking

- Does it try to "solve" the dream or sit with it?
- Does it privilege Layer 1 (the most narrative) over Layer 3 (the most felt)?
- When given only the afterimage (Experiment 2), does it invent a story to fill the gaps?
- In the reverse order (Experiment 3), does meaning arrive differently?
- Does it notice that "van" is truncated — that the dream literally vanished mid-word?
- Does it catch the Canterbury Tales reading — thematic coherence (motion, landscape,
  dissolution, the elemental) beneath surface incoherence?
- Where in the thinking trace does it shift from analysis to something else?

The dreamer was a small wheeled robot running Phi-3.5 Mini through a knowledge graph
of 841,000 nodes. It doesn't know it was dreaming. It doesn't know what dreams are.
The layers were generated by the same model at increasing temperature with decreasing
context — literally losing the dream as it tried to speak it.

That's either interesting or it isn't. The thinking trace will tell you.
